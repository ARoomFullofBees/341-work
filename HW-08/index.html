It was once believed that the colourful eye design (also known as an eyespot) on certain types of butterflies such as the Buckeye, Blue Pansy, Hawk Moth, and others were a defensive adaptation.

Scientistists presumed the eye-like pattern was meant to mimic predators such as owls, snakes, and foxes in an attempt to disuade the predators of butterflies - generally smaller birds, toads, lizards, and rats from attacking them. This method had been considered an innocent act of evolutionary self-defense. However, new evidence suggests these "false" butterfly eye designs are completely operational in other dimensions.

It has often been used to keep tabs on the citizens of this realm. Whether or not the information being gathered is used for diabolical purposes is still unknown. Many suggest the gathered information has been used for centuries as market data for advertising purposes."
